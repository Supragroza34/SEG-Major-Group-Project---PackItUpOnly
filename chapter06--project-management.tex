\section{Project Planning}
At the start of the project, the team held an initial planning session to discuss potential project ideas and define the overall goals and scope. Several possible system concepts were discussed by the group. After evaluating the feasibility of each idea and considering the project requirements and available timeframe, the team conducted a vote to select the most suitable option. As a result, the team agreed to develop a ticketing system as the final project.

Once the project topic had been decided, the team began outlining the structure and functionality of the system. Diagrams and flowcharts were created to visualise the overall system process and to better understand how different components of the system would interact. These diagrams helped the team organise the development process and identify the core features required for the system.

During this stage, the team also focused on identifying the Minimum Viable Product (MVP). By defining the essential features that were necessary for the system to function, the team was able to prioritise development tasks and ensure that the most important components would be completed within the project deadline.

A basic project timeline was then developed to organise the work into different phases, including planning, development, testing and documentation. Breaking the project into stages allowed the team to approach the work in a structured manner and helped ensure that progress was made consistently throughout the development process.
